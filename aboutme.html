Introduction


In this page, you will have the ability to get to know me better as a person, and not as a working machine. You will be able to have an insight of what I like to do, how I am outside school, and what I've done over the years that has helped me grow as a person. I hope you enjoy it! Thank you!

Hobbies


In my free time my favorite thing to do is listen to music, I love discovering new tunes to listen to. When people ask me what my favorite band is, I don't/can't seem to answer that, as I firmly believe that each song has the ability of being great, even if the artists aren't so.
Another of my hobbies is playing videogames. I absolutely LOVE videogames, and not in the conventional way. Not only do I like to play and learn, but I am intrigued by the programming behind them. In my free time I normally listen to music while trying to figure out how the algorithms of a certain "boss battle" work.
Finally, what I would consider my other big hobby is (as cliché as it sounds) learning. Yes! I know, so unoriginal and very used already. Nevertheless it's true, I enjoy learning very much. I don't limit myself to learn about a single subject... if I find or read about something interesting, the next thing I know is that I've spent the last 3 hours researching about bananas. To be honest, I like to research, I take my time and I am very thorough, which can come in very handy when programming or working with maths.

Extracurricular Activities


I was enrolled in extracurricular classes such as piano lessons and drama class. As Paul Klee once said, “One eye sees, the other feels.” I have fun when I play, I have fun when I act. Those small details, each different key or each different movement, involved in these activities, have helped me let go of my problems and hold on to the good feelings.
I’ve also had many different significant experiences regarding sport-related extracurricular activities. One of the things I love to do is to swim. I was part of a swimming team back in Monterrey, I got to participate in a National competition and several state and regional competitions. I was also part of an American Football team back in Monterrey, and was invited to play several times to an cup here in the US.

Charity Work


For the years 2013-2015 I was part of a small group of people that went into different orphanages and resting homes in order to help in whatever thing they needed. 3 hours a week on Saturdays, 7 weeks per semester. This included: Casa Hogar Douglas, Asilo Virgen de Guadalupe, and Casa Hogar para Ancianitos María Esperanza Nuestra.

International Experiences


During the summer of 2015, I went to study abroad in Heidelberg, Germany. I took for two months an intensive German course, 8 hours a day 5 days a week. It was a really fun experience, I got to meet a lot of people from around the world. I am still in contact with most of them, friends from India, Spain, Korea, and Germany. It was a great opportunity to interact with other cultures, and to learn to work with others.
